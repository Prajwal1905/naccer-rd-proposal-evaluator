# Science & Technology (S&T) Grant Funding Guidelines
## Coal/Lignite S&T Programme — Ministry of Coal / CMPDI-NaCCER
### Version: FY2025-26 (Reference Document for Financial Compliance Evaluation)

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

These guidelines govern the financial appraisal of research proposals submitted under the
Science & Technology (S&T) Grant-in-Aid Scheme for the Coal/Lignite sector, administered by
CMPDI (a Miniratna company under Coal India Limited) on behalf of the Ministry of Coal (MoC).
All proposals must comply with the budget caps, justification requirements, and disbursement
conditions described herein. Any proposal that violates these provisions must be flagged during
financial compliance review.

---

## 2. Project Duration Limits

- 2.1 The normal duration of an S&T project shall not exceed **36 months (3 years)**.
- 2.2 Projects proposing a duration beyond 36 months require an explicit justification note
  and will be flagged for committee-level review of feasibility.
- 2.3 Minimum project duration eligible for funding is **12 months**; proposals below this
  threshold must justify why a shorter timeframe is adequate given the proposed scope.

---

## 3. Overall Budget Ceiling

- 3.1 The total project cost (TPC) for a single S&T proposal shall not exceed **INR 2.00 Crore**
  for projects led by academic/research institutions, and **INR 3.50 Crore** for projects with
  industry/CIL subsidiary co-execution involving pilot-scale field trials.
- 3.2 Any proposal with a TPC exceeding these ceilings must include a cost-sharing arrangement
  (institutional contribution or co-funding from another agency) covering at least the excess
  amount, clearly stated in the budget.
- 3.3 Proposals must state TPC in INR Lakhs/Crore; budgets presented only in USD or without
  currency units will be flagged for clarification.

---

## 4. Budget Head-wise Norms (Percentage of Total Project Cost)

| Budget Head | Permissible Ceiling (% of TPC) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 4.1 Manpower (JRF/SRF/Project Associates/RA) | Up to 40% | Must specify number of positions, designation, and monthly emoluments as per applicable DST/CSIR norms |
| 4.2 Equipment & Capital Expenditure | Up to 30% | Items above INR 5 Lakh each require a separate justification note and at least one comparative quotation |
| 4.3 Consumables | Up to 15% | Must be itemized; lump-sum consumable budgets above INR 10 Lakh without itemization will be flagged |
| 4.4 Contingency & Travel | Up to 8% (combined) | Travel must be linked to specific field visits, conferences, or sample collection trips |
| 4.5 Institutional Overhead | Up to 15% (for academic institutions) / Up to 10% (for CIL/PSU executing agencies) | Calculated on TPC excluding equipment cost |
| 4.6 Contractual/Outsourced Services | Up to 10% | Applicable for specialized testing, drone surveys, sensor fabrication, etc.; must name the agency or state "to be tendered" |

- 4.7 The sum of all budget heads must equal the stated Total Project Cost (TPC). Any
  arithmetic mismatch greater than 2% between the sum of line items and the stated TPC is a
  compliance violation.
- 4.8 Equipment proposed to be procured must be justified as not already available at the
  executing institution; proposals listing "general purpose computing" (laptops, desktops)
  exceeding 5% of TPC require justification.

---

## 5. Manpower Norms

- 5.1 Junior Research Fellow (JRF): INR 31,000/month + HRA as applicable (Year 1-2); enhanced
  to Senior Research Fellow (SRF) rate of INR 35,000/month + HRA from Year 3 onward, subject to
  satisfactory performance review.
- 5.2 Project Associate / Research Associate: INR 47,000 - 54,000/month + HRA, depending on
  qualification (PhD vs. M.Tech).
- 5.3 Field Assistants/Technical Support staff: maximum INR 18,000/month.
- 5.4 Manpower costs that deviate from these norms by more than 10% without explicit
  justification (e.g., institute-specific pay scale circulars) will be flagged.

---

## 6. Equipment Procurement Conditions

- 6.1 All equipment with unit cost exceeding INR 5 Lakh must be accompanied by a brief
  technical justification explaining why the equipment is essential and not available as a
  shared facility.
- 6.2 Equipment procurement should generally be front-loaded to Year 1 or Year 2; equipment
  budgets concentrated in the final year of the project will be flagged as a feasibility/
  utilization concern.
- 6.3 Software licenses (e.g., simulation software, GIS, ML platforms) are classified under
  Equipment if perpetual, or under Consumables/Contingency if annual/subscription-based.

---

## 7. Disbursement Schedule

- 7.1 Funds are released in **annual installments** based on submission and approval of:
  (a) Utilization Certificate (UC) for the previous installment, and
  (b) Annual Progress Report.
- 7.2 The first installment (typically 30-40% of TPC) is released upon signing of the
  Memorandum of Agreement (MoA).
- 7.3 No proposal may request 100% upfront disbursement; proposals structured for full
  upfront payment will be flagged as non-compliant.

---

## 8. Overhead and Institutional Charges

- 8.1 Institutional overhead (as per Section 4.5) is intended to cover administrative costs,
  electricity, and shared infrastructure usage, and is NOT to be itemized further by the
  proposing institution.
- 8.2 CIL subsidiaries and other PSU executing agencies are capped at a lower overhead rate
  (10%) compared to academic institutions (15%), reflecting their existing infrastructure.

---

## 9. Ineligible / Restricted Expenditure Items

The following items are NOT permissible under the S&T grant and will be flagged if present:

- 9.1 Construction or civil work expenditure exceeding 5% of TPC (minor site preparation for
  field trials is permissible up to this limit; full building construction is not eligible).
- 9.2 Purchase of land or vehicles (two-wheelers, cars) for personal/staff use.
- 9.3 Honoraria to Principal Investigators (PIs) or Co-PIs from CIL/Government institutions
  who already draw a regular salary (no additional PI honorarium is permitted for
  government/PSU employees).
- 9.4 Foreign travel exceeding INR 5 Lakh per project without prior approval from the
  Ministry of Coal's S&T Standing Committee.
- 9.5 Retrospective expenditure (costs incurred before the MoA signing date).

---

## 10. Required Financial Documentation Checklist

A proposal's financial section is considered compliant only if it includes ALL of the following:

- 10.1 Year-wise (annual) break-up of the budget across all heads, not just a single
  consolidated total.
- 10.2 Justification narrative for Manpower, Equipment, and Contractual Services heads.
- 10.3 A statement of any co-funding/cost-sharing arrangement, if TPC exceeds the relevant
  ceiling under Section 3.
- 10.4 Explicit currency denomination (INR Lakh/Crore) for all figures.
- 10.5 A summary table where the sum of head-wise totals reconciles with the stated TPC.

---

## 11. Flagging Severity Levels (for Automated Review)

| Severity | Criteria |
|---|---|
| HIGH | Exceeds overall TPC ceiling without cost-sharing; ineligible expenditure items present (Section 9); arithmetic mismatch >2% (Section 4.7); 100% upfront disbursement request |
| MEDIUM | Any single budget head exceeds its percentage ceiling (Section 4); manpower rates deviate >10% from norms without justification; missing year-wise breakup |
| LOW | Missing currency units; missing justification narratives for high-value equipment; software licensing miscategorized |

---

## 12. Notes for Reviewers

These guidelines are reference norms for FY2025-26 and are subject to periodic revision by the
Ministry of Coal's S&T Standing Committee. Proposals from institutions with formally approved
deviation circulars (e.g., higher manpower rates under a specific government pay commission
update) should be flagged for manual review rather than automatically rejected, since the
automated system may not have access to the latest circulars.
