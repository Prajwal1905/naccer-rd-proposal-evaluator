# CIL / Ministry of Coal — Priority Research Areas for S&T Funding
## Reference Document for Feasibility & Relevance Scoring (FY2025-26)

This document lists the priority thrust areas identified by Coal India Limited (CIL) and the
Ministry of Coal (MoC) for funding under the S&T Grant-in-Aid programme, as compiled by
CMPDI/NaCCER. Proposals are evaluated for relevance based on alignment with these areas. A
proposal addressing multiple priority areas, or addressing a HIGH-priority area with strong
methodological fit, should receive a higher feasibility/relevance score.

---

## A. HIGH PRIORITY AREAS

### A1. Mine Safety & Disaster Prevention
- Early-warning systems for roof fall, rib fall, and strata movement in underground mines
- Spontaneous heating/fire detection and suppression in goaf and coal stockyards
- Gas (methane, CO) monitoring, real-time worker tracking, and emergency communication systems
- Slope stability monitoring and landslide/subsidence prediction in opencast mines
- Blast design optimization to reduce fly rock, ground vibration, and noise impact

### A2. Environmental Management & Mine Closure
- Mine void/overburden dump reclamation and afforestation strategies
- Acid mine drainage (AMD) treatment and water quality management
- Air quality monitoring and dust suppression technologies around mining areas
- Scientific mine closure planning, including post-closure land-use and water body management
- Carbon sequestration and climate impact assessment of mining and rehabilitation activities

### A3. Automation, Digitalization & AI in Mining
- AI/ML applications for production planning, equipment health monitoring, and predictive
  maintenance
- Autonomous and tele-remote operation of mining equipment (HEMM, longwall, continuous miners)
- Digital twin platforms for mine planning, ventilation, and operations
- Drone/remote sensing applications for survey, monitoring, and reserve estimation
- Data analytics and decision-support dashboards for mine management

### A4. Coal Beneficiation & Utilization
- Beneficiation technologies to reduce ash content for power and non-power sectors
- Coal-to-chemicals, coal gasification (surface and underground), and clean coal technologies
- Utilization of fly ash, coal washery rejects, and overburden material in construction
  materials and infrastructure
- Coal Bed Methane (CBM) and mine gas capture/utilization, including from abandoned workings

---

## B. MEDIUM PRIORITY AREAS

### B1. Geological & Reserve Assessment
- Improved exploration techniques for deep and difficult-to-access coal seams
- Coal quality assessment and rapid characterization methods
- 3D geological modeling and resource estimation tools

### B2. Equipment, Materials & Process Improvement
- Indigenous development of mining machinery components and spares
- Wear-resistant materials for HEMM and conveyor systems
- Energy efficiency improvements in mine ventilation, pumping, and haulage systems

### B3. Human Factors & Occupational Health
- Occupational health studies related to dust exposure (pneumoconiosis), noise, and heat stress
- Ergonomic design of mining equipment cabins and workstations
- Training and skill development technologies (VR/AR-based simulators)

---

## C. LOWER PRIORITY / GENERAL INTEREST AREAS

### C1. General IT Infrastructure
- Generic enterprise software (HR systems, payroll, generic ERP modules) without a clear
  mining-domain-specific innovation component
- General-purpose data storage or networking infrastructure projects not tied to a specific
  operational or safety use case

### C2. Pure Basic Research
- Fundamental geological or chemical research without a clear pathway to application in
  CIL/coal-sector operations within the project timeframe

---

## D. CROSS-CUTTING EVALUATION NOTES

- D1. Proposals that combine AI/ML or digital technologies (Area A3) with a domain priority
  area (A1, A2, A4) are considered particularly well-aligned with current S&T thrust, as they
  represent both technological modernization and core operational need.
- D2. Proposals proposing technologies already piloted successfully under previous S&T
  projects (see reference proposal database) should clearly differentiate their novelty —
  e.g., extension to new geology, improved accuracy, lower cost, or new deployment context —
  otherwise relevance score should be moderated even if the topic area is high priority.
- D3. Proposals must specify which CIL subsidiary, coalfield, or mine type (UG/OC) the proposed
  work will be validated in or applies to; proposals lacking this specificity receive a lower
  feasibility score due to unclear operational pathway.
- D4. Multi-disciplinary proposals (e.g., combining environmental monitoring with ML-based
  prediction) are encouraged and should not be penalized for spanning multiple priority
  categories, provided objectives remain coherent and achievable within the proposed duration.

---

## E. SCORING GUIDANCE (for LLM Feasibility/Relevance Module)

When generating a relevance score (0-100) and reasoning, consider:

1. **Priority Area Match (40%)** — Does the proposal's research area fall under HIGH (A),
   MEDIUM (B), or LOWER (C) priority categories above?
2. **Operational Specificity (25%)** — Does the proposal name specific CIL subsidiaries,
   coalfields, or mine sites for validation/deployment (per D3)?
3. **Technology-Domain Alignment (20%)** — Does it combine digital/AI methods with a domain
   problem per D1, indicating strong modernization alignment?
4. **Differentiation from Prior Work (15%)** — Per D2, does the proposal articulate what is
   new relative to similar past S&T projects, or does it largely replicate prior scope?

A proposal scoring below 40 overall should generally be flagged as LOW relevance with specific
reasoning on which dimension(s) it falls short.
